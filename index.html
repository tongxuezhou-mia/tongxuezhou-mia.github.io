About Me

I am an Associate Professor in the College of Information Science and Technology at Hangzhou Normal University. My research focuses on deep learning, image processing, medical image analysis, and multimodal data fusion, with a particular interest in reliable models for MRI, CT, PET, and other medical imaging data.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from INSA Rouen in 2022. From 2017 to 2018, I was a visiting scholar at Fraunhofer Darmstadt. My work has appeared in journals and conferences including Medical Image Analysis, IEEE Transactions on Image Processing, Information Fusion, Pattern Recognition, MICCAI, and ICPR.

Research Interests

Reliable Medical Image Analysis

Deep models for automatic lesion, abnormal-structure, and anatomical-feature recognition across MRI, CT, and PET imaging.

Multimodal Fusion

Fusion of complementary sensor and imaging modalities to improve diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment planning.

Brain Tumor Segmentation

Missing-modality MRI segmentation, recurrence-location prediction, uncertainty quantification, and boundary-aware modeling.

Computer Vision

Image classification, object detection, semantic segmentation, visual tracking, and feature representation learning.
